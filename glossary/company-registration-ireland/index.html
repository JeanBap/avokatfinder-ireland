Company Registration

Company Registration Ireland – CRO Process and Requirements

Registering a company in Ireland is managed by the Companies Registration Office (CRO), the statutory body responsible for incorporating companies and maintaining the public register of Irish companies. Ireland's corporate law framework — governed primarily by the Companies Act 2014 — is considered one of the most modern and flexible in the EU, which is a significant factor in Ireland's attractiveness as a European business base.

The most common entity is the Private Company Limited by Shares (LTD) — a simplified structure introduced by the Companies Act 2014 that can have a single director, a single shareholder, and does not require a formal memorandum of association. The LTD has a one-document constitution and full legal capacity to carry on any lawful business.

Incorporation requires submitting a Form A1 (incorporating document) and the company's constitution to the CRO, accompanied by the registration fee (€50 online, €100 paper). The A1 sets out the company name, registered office address (which must be in Ireland), director details, and share structure. CRO typically processes online applications within three to five working days.

Irish companies must have at least one EEA-resident director (unless they hold a Section 137 bond of €25,000 as security against potential fines). They must also have an Irish-registered company secretary (who may be a director). Registered office address services are available from company formation agents for businesses without a physical Irish presence.

Annual filing obligations include submission of annual returns (Form B1 and financial statements) to the CRO, and corporation tax returns to Revenue. Late annual returns result in late filing penalties and can ultimately lead to the company being struck off the register. An active Irish limited company's filings are publicly searchable on the CRO online register.

⚠ Common MistakeUsing a director's home address as the registered office without understanding that it becomes publicly searchable on the CRO register. Privacy-conscious directors should use a registered office service.
💡 Expert TipIncorporate online via core.ie (the CRO's online platform) rather than paper application. Online applications are processed faster, and the digital company file is immediately accessible for subsequent filings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a non-Irish resident set up a company in Ireland?

Yes, but at least one director must be EEA-resident, or the company must provide a Section 137 bond. Many non-resident founders appoint a local nominee director or use a formation agent who provides a qualifying director.

How long does company incorporation take in Ireland?

Typically three to five business days for online applications via the CRO's Core system. Expedited registration (24 hours) is not officially offered by the CRO but some agents offer same-day incorporation through advance preparation.

What is the difference between LTD and DAC in Ireland?

An LTD is a general-purpose private company with unlimited objects. A DAC (Designated Activity Company) has specific objects listed in its constitution, similar to the old model articles. DACs are used where lenders or investors require a restricted-purpose vehicle.
