Title Deed

Title Deed Ireland – Land Registry Folio vs Unregistered Title

A title deed is the legal document establishing ownership of real property. In Ireland, property title exists in two parallel systems: registered title (recorded as a folio in the Land Registry, maintained by the Property Registration Authority) and unregistered title (evidenced by a bundle of historical title deeds held privately or by lenders).

Registered title — maintained in the Land Registry — is the modern, authoritative record. A Land Registry folio shows the property boundaries (on the accompanying filed plan), the registered owner, and any mortgages, easements, rights of way, or other burdens affecting the property. The folio is a state guarantee of title, meaning the State compensates any person who suffers loss due to an error in the register.

Unregistered title — recorded in the Registry of Deeds — is older and requires reviewing a chain of title deeds going back at least 15 years (or, for older properties, significantly longer) to establish a good title. Most new transactions involving unregistered land must be registered in the Land Registry within six months of the deed of transfer, meaning unregistered title is gradually being converted.

Key Facts

Land RegistryState-guaranteed, folio-based system (PRA)
Registry of DeedsOlder unregistered title recording
Folio contentsOwner, boundaries, burdens, mortgages
New deed registrationMandatory within 6 months of transfer
Title standardGood and marketable, free from encumbrances

Buyers should confirm whether the property is registered or unregistered early in the conveyancing process. Unregistered title is more complex and time-consuming to investigate, often requiring searches in both the Registry of Deeds and the Registry of Judgments and the Land Charges Register. An incomplete title chain can prevent a sale from completing.

Good and marketable title is the standard required for completion. This means the seller can convey ownership free from undisclosed encumbrances and with full authority to sell. If title defects are found — such as missing planning permissions, undisclosed right-of-ways, or title gaps — they must be resolved or indemnified before the sale can complete.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming a Land Registry folio means no further investigation is needed. Folios record registered burdens but may not capture all informal rights or planning compliance issues affecting the property.
💡 Expert TipAsk your solicitor to request an up-to-date official copy of the folio and filed plan from the PRA before contracts are exchanged. This costs only a few euros but confirms the current state of the registered title.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Land Registry folio number?

A unique reference number assigned to each registered property in Ireland, used to identify the property's record in the Land Registry database.

Do I get the title deeds when I buy a property?

For registered title, the solicitor updates the folio in the Land Registry in your name. Physical title deeds are less relevant for registered land; your evidence of ownership is the Land Registry folio. For unregistered land, physical deeds are still significant.

What happens if title deeds are lost?

Lost title deeds for unregistered land can be reconstructed using the Registry of Deeds and other historical records, but this is time-consuming and expensive. First registration in the Land Registry is typically sought as part of any transaction involving lost deeds.
