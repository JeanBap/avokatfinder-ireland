Revenue Commissioners

Revenue Commissioners Ireland – Role in Property Transactions

The Revenue Commissioners (Revenue) is Ireland's national tax and customs authority, responsible for assessing and collecting all taxes including Stamp Duty, Capital Gains Tax (CGT), Local Property Tax (LPT), and Capital Acquisitions Tax (CAT) — all of which are directly relevant to property transactions in Ireland.

For property buyers, the most immediate interaction with Revenue is the Stamp Duty return. Stamp Duty must be filed electronically on Revenue's ROS (Revenue Online Service) system within 44 days of the execution of the deed of transfer. The solicitor acting for the buyer typically handles this filing, but the liability rests with the buyer.

Sellers interact with Revenue primarily through CGT returns and, where applicable, CG50A applications (clearance certificates for non-residents). Revenue also administers the Local Property Tax, which is assessed on property owners each year based on self-assessed banded values. Outstanding LPT must be cleared before a property can be sold.

Online portalmyAccount (individuals) / ROS (businesses)

Revenue operates a myAccount portal for individuals and ROS for businesses and solicitors. Property transactions increasingly generate automatic data-sharing between solicitors, the Property Registration Authority (PRA), and Revenue, meaning discrepancies between declared consideration and market value are increasingly flagged.

Revenue's Stamp Duty team has the power to raise enquiries and assessments up to four years after a return is filed (or indefinitely in cases of fraud). Buyers who have claimed Stamp Duty exemptions — such as the residential development relief — should maintain documentation for at least six years.

⚠ Common MistakeMissing the 44-day Stamp Duty filing deadline. Interest accrues daily, and late filing can also delay land registration since Revenue clearance is required before the PRA processes the transfer.
💡 Expert TipInstruct your solicitor to file the Stamp Duty return and obtain Revenue clearance well before the 44-day deadline. In practice, most solicitors file within days of completion, but confirming the timeline with your solicitor is good practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I pay Local Property Tax when selling?

Your solicitor will verify LPT compliance as part of the closing process. Outstanding LPT is typically cleared from the sale proceeds. The seller must also ensure the buyer is registered as the new LPT owner.

Does Revenue always audit property transactions?

No, but Revenue uses risk-based selection and data analytics to identify transactions where consideration appears below market value or where relief claims may be incorrect.

What is a PPS number and why does Revenue need it?

A Personal Public Service (PPS) number is Ireland's national identifier for individuals. It is required for all Revenue filings, including Stamp Duty returns and CGT self-assessment.
