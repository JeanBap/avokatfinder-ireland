Stamp Duty (Residential)

Stamp Duty Ireland Residential Property – Rates, Exemptions, First-Time Buyers

Stamp Duty is a tax on legal instruments — including deeds of transfer — levied by Revenue on property purchases in Ireland. For residential property, the rate is 1% on the first €1,000,000 of consideration and 2% on any amount above that threshold. It is payable by the buyer and must be filed and paid electronically within 44 days of executing the deed of transfer.

First-time buyers are entitled to a refund of stamp duty to the extent that the consideration does not exceed €500,000 under the Help to Buy scheme (a separate income tax refund scheme, not a stamp duty relief per se). However, a stamp duty refund is available on new homes purchased from a builder where the buyer claims the scheme. Conditions include owner-occupancy and income limits.

Commercial property attracts a higher rate of 7.5% (reduced from 10% from Budget 2020 onwards for acquisitions of commercial property used for a residential conversion). Buying a residential property through a company rather than personally also attracts higher rates and may trigger additional SDLT-equivalent charges, making personal ownership generally more tax-efficient for standard residential investments.

Key Facts

Residential rate1% (≤€1m) / 2% (>€1m)
Commercial rate7.5%
Filing deadline44 days from deed execution
Filing methodRevenue Online Service (ROS)
ExemptionSpouse/civil partner transfers

Certain transfers are exempt from or subject to reduced stamp duty: transfers between spouses or civil partners (exempt), transfers to charities (exempt), and transfers of shares in a property-owning company (1% on share consideration, potentially triggering anti-avoidance provisions). Transfers to children attracting consanguinity relief were phased out and the standard rate now applies.

The 44-day filing obligation runs from the date of execution of the deed — not from completion or registration. The solicitor files the Stamp Duty return on ROS, pays the duty electronically, and receives a PRA stamp or certificate of duty paid, which must be presented for land registration. Missing the deadline triggers Revenue interest surcharges that can be significant on large transactions.

⚠ Common MistakeConfusing the 44-day deadline as running from the date of land registration. It runs from execution of the deed — often completion day — which may be weeks before registration is completed.
💡 Expert TipOn large transactions over €1 million, ask your solicitor to calculate the exact stamp duty liability at heads-of-terms stage so it can be factored into your financial planning and mortgage application.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is stamp duty refundable if a property sale falls through after the deed is executed?

Revenue will refund stamp duty in limited circumstances, including where a transaction is unwound by agreement. A formal application and evidence are required.

Do I pay stamp duty on a new-build purchase?

Yes. Stamp duty applies to new-build residential purchases at the standard rates. The Help to Buy scheme provides a separate income tax/DIRT refund but does not reduce the stamp duty liability.

Is stamp duty charged on the deposit or the full purchase price?

Stamp duty is charged on the full purchase price (consideration) as stated in the deed of transfer, not merely the deposit paid at contract exchange.
