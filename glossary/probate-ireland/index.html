Probate

Probate Ireland – Applying for Grant of Probate Explained

Probate in Ireland is the legal process of proving that a will is valid and authorising the executor to administer the deceased's estate. It is managed by the Probate Office of the High Court (in Dublin) or via District Probate Registries across the country. A grant of probate — or grant of administration if there is no will — is required to sell or transfer property that formed part of the deceased's estate.

When a person dies with a valid will, their executor applies for a grant of probate by filing the original will, the death certificate, a sworn Oath of Executor, and the Inland Revenue Affidavit (Form CA24) with the Probate Office. The CA24 lists all assets and liabilities and establishes the value of the estate for Capital Acquisitions Tax (CAT) purposes.

If the deceased died without a will (intestate), a grant of administration is issued instead to the next of kin, following the order of priority set out in the Succession Act 1965. The administrator has the same authority as an executor but is bound by the strict intestacy rules for distributing the estate.

Key Facts

Governing legislationSuccession Act 1965
Application bodyProbate Office / District Probate Registry
Key formInland Revenue Affidavit (CA24)
Intestacy orderSpouse, children, parents, siblings
Typical timeline3–12 months

The probate process typically takes three to twelve months depending on the complexity of the estate, the volume of applications at the Probate Office, and whether the will is contested. Personal application is possible for straightforward estates, but most families use a solicitor given the documentation requirements and potential tax implications.

Property cannot be sold or transferred out of an estate until probate is granted. If a property sale is time-critical (for example, a contracted sale is pending), executors should initiate the probate application as soon as possible after death. Delays in grant of probate regularly cause property sale completions to collapse.

⚠ Common MistakeWaiting until a property sale is agreed before initiating probate. Probate can take months, and agreed sales regularly fall through while waiting for a grant to issue.
💡 Expert TipIf the estate includes property likely to be sold, instruct a solicitor to begin the probate application within weeks of the death, not months. The Revenue CA24 form is the most time-consuming document — gather asset valuations and financial statements early.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need probate to sell a jointly owned property?

If the property was held as joint tenants, it passes automatically to the surviving owner by survivorship and probate is not required. If held as tenants in common, the deceased's share forms part of the estate and probate is required.

How much does probate cost in Ireland?

Probate Office fees are calculated on the estate value (free up to €20,000, then scaled). Solicitor fees are additional and typically range from €2,000 upward depending on complexity.

Can a will be contested during probate?

Yes. Contentious probate proceedings can be initiated in the High Court by anyone with legal standing, including disappointed beneficiaries or potential dependants claiming under Section 117 of the Succession Act.
