Tenancy Agreement

Tenancy Agreement Ireland – Rights, RTB Registration, and Rent Rules

A tenancy agreement in Ireland is the contract between a landlord and tenant governing a residential letting. It can be written or verbal, though written agreements are strongly recommended. All tenancy agreements are subject to the Residential Tenancies Act 2004 (as amended), which sets minimum rights and obligations that cannot be contracted out of regardless of what the agreement says.

All residential tenancies must be registered with the Residential Tenancies Board (RTB) within one month of the tenancy commencing. Registration costs €40 per tenancy (online). Failure to register is an offence and prevents the landlord from referring certain disputes to the RTB. In practice, unregistered tenancies also attract Revenue scrutiny.

Rent increases in Rent Pressure Zones (RPZs) — currently covering virtually all of Ireland — are capped at the lower of 2% per year or general inflation (HICP). Landlords outside RPZs can set market rent but must still give 90 days' notice of any rent review. Rent review notices must contain specific information prescribed by the RTB or they are invalid.

After six months' continuous occupation, a tenant acquires a Part 4 tenancy — a statutory right to remain for up to six years in total. Landlords can only terminate a Part 4 tenancy on specific statutory grounds: sale of the property, owner occupancy need, breach by the tenant, or significant renovation. Notice periods range from 28 days (under 6 months) to 224 days (over 8 years).

Disputes between landlords and tenants are heard by the RTB's Dispute Resolution Service, not the civil courts. The RTB issues adjudication determinations and, if necessary, tribunal decisions, which are enforceable in the Circuit Court. Solicitors are not permitted to attend RTB adjudications, keeping costs lower than court proceedings.

⚠ Common MistakeIssuing a rent increase notice without including all RTB-prescribed content. Invalid notices are void, and landlords may lose the benefit of the intended rent review entirely.
💡 Expert TipUse the RTB's prescribed notice of rent review template (available on rtb.ie) for every rent review. Customised notices frequently omit a required element and are later found invalid in RTB proceedings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a landlord terminate a tenancy in Ireland without giving a reason?

Not after six months. Before six months, a landlord can terminate without stating a ground (with appropriate notice). After six months, one of the statutory termination grounds must apply and be stated in the notice.

What is the deposit cap in Ireland?

Since 2022, landlords can charge a maximum of one month's rent as a deposit for a standard residential tenancy.

Does a tenancy agreement have to be in writing?

No, but the landlord must provide the tenant with a written statement of the key terms within one month of the tenancy starting, regardless of whether a formal written agreement exists.
